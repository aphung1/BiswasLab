WHO WE ARE

Under the direction of Dr. Debabrata Biswas, our mission is to improve safety by understanding the bacterial pathogens behind foodborne illnesses. We aim to uncover novel mechanisms to control the spread and colonization of these bacteria in food processing plants and animal reservoirs. Our expertise falls along:

  • Bacteriology
  • Foodborne Pathogens
  • Vaccine
  • Alternative Therapeutics
  • Alternative Growth Promoters

Control of foodborne bacteria through use of plant-derived phenolic compounds

Plants remain a prime source for novel compounds with bioactive potential, many of which can be found in the form of phenolic acids. These have demonstrated antimicrobial potential against Salmonella enterica serovar Typhimurium, while also impairing specific virulence factors. Read more>>

A Highly Effective Bacteriophage-1252 to Control Multiple Serovars of Salmonella enterica

Bacteriophage (phage) therapies have emerged as a promising alternative to controlling bacterial pathogens. However, the limitation on the lysis ability of most phages is their species-specificity to the bacterium. In this study, Salmonella bacteriophage-1252 (phage-1252) was isolated and found to have the highest lytic activity against multiple serovars of S. enterica, including Typhimurium, Enteritidis, Newport, Heidelberg, Kentucky, and Gallinarum. Read more>>

Causative agents for fowl typhoid and pullorum disease in poultry and approach to control

Both antibiotic-free conventional and pasture poultry farmers are facing the re-emergence of bacterial diseases, including fowl typhoid and pullorum disease. These diseases cause higher mortality rate in the birds and lead to non-profitable poultry farming. In this study, we have investigated S. Gallinarum and S.
